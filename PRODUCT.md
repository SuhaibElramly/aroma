# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The shop owner or a single trusted manager for Aroma Shop, a perfume and beauty boutique in Benghazi, Libya. They use this admin tool at a desk or at the counter, during business hours (10am–10pm), typically checking overnight orders, updating product listings, and monitoring sales. They're not a technical user — they need immediate clarity on what needs attention, zero friction for common tasks, and confidence that the data on screen is current and correct.

## Product Purpose

Internal admin panel for Aroma Shop (aromashop.ly). Manages the full catalog (products, variants, brands, categories), processes orders, and tracks customers. Success looks like: the manager can find what they need in two clicks, act on it without confusion, and return to the floor.

## Brand Personality

Warm, precise, unhurried. The admin is the behind-the-scenes counterpart to a boutique that takes pride in how it presents itself. It should feel like a well-kept stockroom: organized, purposeful, nothing out of place.

Three words: composed, considered, warm.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with teal/purple color schemes and hero metric cards.
- Dark "hacker" admin panels that feel cold and technical.
- Cluttered WordPress-style admin UIs with too many panels and nested menus.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity first.** Every element on screen should earn its presence. If it doesn't help the manager act or decide, remove it.
2. **Warm precision.** The brand is warm but professional. The admin should feel like the store's back office — not a startup tool, not a cold enterprise system.
3. **Respect the workflow.** Common actions (checking orders, editing a product) should never require more than two steps.
4. **Trust through consistency.** Uniform spacing, predictable patterns, and quiet motion create confidence. Surprise is a bug, not a feature.
5. **The accent means something.** The warm amber (#B8966E) is the brand color. Use it to signal the one action that matters on a screen — not as decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. The interface is used under variable lighting conditions (back-office to bright retail floor). Sufficient contrast on all interactive elements. Focus states visible. Arabic content (product names) must render cleanly — no letter-spacing on Arabic text.
