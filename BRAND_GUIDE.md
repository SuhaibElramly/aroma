## Voice & Tone

### Overall character
- **Warm, proud, and welcoming** — writes like a trusted local business that genuinely cares.
- **Celebratory without being loud** — uses emojis tastefully (✨❤️📢🌟🎁📍🕘) to add energy, not replace it.
- **Grateful and faith-rooted** — opens important announcements with الحمد لله and uses Islamic phrasing naturally (not forcibly).
- **Community-first** — thanks supporters personally, creates a sense of shared achievement.

### Language
- **Primary language: Arabic (MSA blended with light Libyan/regional tone).** No dialect slang, but warm and approachable, not stiff or formal.
- **Bilingual brand identity:** the brand name and tagline appear in English ("AROMA", "SMELL GOOD, FEEL GOOD") while all communication is in Arabic.
- No letter-spacing on Arabic text.
- Clean punctuation — uses | as a separator in headlines (e.g. `كل ماهو جديد | تسليم فوري`).

## Post Structure Patterns

### Product / New Arrival Post
```
[Headline with emoji] | [key selling point]
مرحباً في أرومـا 🌟
[Product description or offer]
[CTA or delivery note]
```
Example headline: `كل ماهو جديد | تسليم فوري 🎁`

### Announcement / Event Post
```
[Post title in bold-style caps + emojis]
الحمد لله الذي بفضله تتم الصالحات .. ❤️
[Core announcement — proud and warm]
📍 الموقع: [address]
🕘 مواعيد العمل: [hours]
[Personal thank-you note to supporters]
نتطلع إلى زيارتكم قريباً!
```

### Seasonal / Holiday Post
- Ties products to the occasion (e.g. Ramadan gift sets).
- Uses seasonal imagery in Arabic + keeps the Aroma logo visible.
- Example image text pattern: `AROMA — RAMADAN KAREEM`

## Emoji Usage

Used consistently as visual anchors, not decoration:

| Emoji | Context |
|---|---|
| ✨ | Announcements, highlights, launches |
| ❤️ | Gratitude, warmth, closing lines |
| 📢 | News / announcements |
| 🌟 | Welcome lines, featured products |
| 🎁 | Gifts, new arrivals, offers |
| 📍 | Location |
| 🕘 | Working hours |

---

## What to Avoid

- No overly formal MSA (فصحى متكلفة) — keep it readable and warm.
- No slang or heavy Libyan dialect.
- No letter-spacing on Arabic words.
- No excessive emoji stacking (max 2-3 per line).
- Don't skip the gratitude / faith element in major announcements — it's core to the brand's identity.
- Prices are displayed on the website — always show prices clearly on product pages and listings.

## Audience Signals

- Mainly Arabic-speaking Libyans (Benghazi area + online).
- High engagement on product photos (perfume bottles, gift sets).
